# Anti-Gaming Mechanisms

The tokenomics system is designed to make all extraction strategies net-negative. Every mechanism reinforces the others — there is no single point of exploitation.

## Mechanism Table

| # | Mechanism | Protects Against | How |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 1 | Soulbound NFTs (ERC-5192) | Claim sniping, secondary market speculation | `_update()` reverts all transfers. Only mint and burn allowed. |
| 2 | Smooth time-weighted redemption (0->80% over 365d) | Mint-and-flip, impulsive redemption | Linear vesting, not cliff. No way to game a threshold. |
| 3 | Smooth sliding redemption fee (20%->3%) | Short-term extraction, cliff gaming | Continuous decay, not stepped. No advantageous exit window. |
| 4 | 20% vault reserve (baked into pro-rata) | Bank-run vault drainage, race conditions | Deterministic `*0.8` multiplier. Cannot be drained even if all redeem simultaneously. |
| 5 | 48-hour redemption cooldown per address | Bot-driven vault drainage | `lastRedemptionTime` enforced on-chain. |
| 6 | 70% LP burn on mint | Makes ALL gaming cycles net-negative | Irrecoverable cost. You can never get back more than 30% of your mint cost. |
| 7 | `activeSupply`-based pricing | Natural market-driven supply equilibrium | Price drops when holders leave, rises when they join. |
| 8 | Adaptive vault reinvestment ratio | Vault depletion death spiral | Harvest retains 95% when vault is stressed. |
| 9 | Rolling vault target (not ATH) | Whale-inflated target manipulation | Target = 30% of `totalActiveMintCost`, adjusts on every mint/redeem. |
| 10 | Capped caller bounty on triggers | MEV bounty gaming | Hard cap + max 5% bps limit on bounty. |
| 11 | Minimum LP balance threshold for trigger | Dust griefing on permissionless calls | Configurable floor prevents processing worthless amounts. |
| 12 | LP auto-registration via Factory validation | Operational bottleneck bypass | Anyone can register a legitimate pair — no owner dependency. |

## Attack Analysis

### Attack 1: Redeem-to-Cheapen-Then-Remint

**Strategy**: Redeem NFTs to lower `activeSupply` (cheaper mints), then re-mint at the lower price.

**Why it fails**:

```
Round 1: Mint 10 NFTs at total cost 10 LP
  -> 7 LP burned forever (70%)
  -> 3 LP to vault (30%)

Round 2: Redeem all 10 after 365 days
  -> Best case: recover ~2.91 LP (29.1% of cost)
  -> Net loss so far: 7.09 LP

Round 3: Remint at lower price (activeSupply = 0)
  -> Must spend another ~10 LP at base price
  -> Another 7 LP burned immediately
```

**Result**: Each cycle destroys ~70% of the LP involved. After two rounds, you've spent ~20 LP and burned ~14 LP permanently. The price discount from lowering `activeSupply` cannot compensate for the 70% burn.

### Attack 2: Claim Sniping (Just-in-Time Minting)

**Strategy**: Mint right before a fee distribution, claim, then redeem.

**Why it fails**:

1. **Soulbound** — Can't buy an existing NFT from someone else
2. **Mint cost** — Must pay the full bonding curve price in LP tokens
3. **70% burned** — Immediate 70% loss on mint
4. **Time weight 0%** — Cannot redeem anything on day 0
5. **20% fee** — Even if you could redeem, you'd pay the maximum exit fee
6. **Fee share** — Your share of one distribution cycle is a tiny fraction of mint cost

**Math**: If triggering distributes $100 across 50 holders, each share is $2. Your mint cost for 1 NFT might be $50+. You'd need 25+ distribution cycles just to break even on the 30% that went to vault, ignoring the 70% burn entirely.

### Attack 3: Vault Drainage (Bank Run)

**Strategy**: Coordinate mass redemptions to drain the vault before others can exit.

**Why it fails**:

The 20% reserve is **not a threshold** — it's built into the formula:

```
vaultProRata = (vaultBalance * 0.8 * mintCostLP) / totalActiveMintCost
```

Simulation with 10 holders, each minted for 1 LP, vault has 3 LP:

| Redeemer | Vault Before | Pro-Rata | Fee (3%) | Net | Vault After |
|----------|-------------|----------|----------|-----|-------------|
| #1 | 3.00 | 0.240 | 0.007 | 0.233 | 2.764 |
| #2 | 2.764 | 0.222 | 0.007 | 0.216 | 2.545 |
| #3 | 2.545 | 0.204 | 0.006 | 0.198 | 2.341 |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| #10 | 0.885 | 0.071 | 0.002 | 0.069 | 0.814 |

After all 10 redeem: **0.814 LP remains** in the vault. The 20% reserve held. No one got an unfair advantage — each redeemer got proportionally less as the vault shrank.

### Attack 4: MEV on Trigger Bounty

**Strategy**: Front-run the fee listener to steal the trigger bounty.

**Why it's limited**:

- Bounty is capped at `triggerBountyCap` (absolute maximum)
- Bounty is max 5% of output (configurable, default 1%)
- Anyone can trigger — this is a feature, not a bug. MEV searchers triggering early is fine because the protocol wants timely distributions.
- No advantage from ordering — the accumulator distributes the same regardless of trigger timing.

### Attack 5: Whale Manipulation of Vault Target

**Strategy**: Mint many NFTs to inflate `totalActiveMintCost`, making the vault appear underfunded, preventing harvest distributions.

**Why it fails**:

- The vault target is `30% of totalActiveMintCost`
- When a whale mints, 30% of their LP goes to the vault, exactly matching the target increase
- The vault's health ratio stays roughly constant
- And the whale has burned 70% of their LP permanently

## Soulbound Enforcement

The `_update()` override in DSFONFTv3 blocks all transfers:

```solidity
function _update(address to, uint256 tokenId, address auth)
    internal override(ERC721) returns (address)
{
    address from = _ownerOf(tokenId);
    if (from != address(0) && to != address(0)) {
        revert("DSFO: soulbound, transfers disabled");
    }
    // ... mint/burn allowed
}
```

Only mint (from=0) and burn (to=0) are allowed. This prevents:

- Buying NFTs just before a fee distribution
- Selling NFTs to avoid redemption penalties
- Secondary market speculation disconnected from protocol participation
- Flash-loan-funded claim extraction

## Why Smooth Curves Beat Cliff Vesting

Both the time weight (0->80%) and the sliding fee (20->3%) use **smooth linear curves** instead of cliff schedules. This is intentional:

- **No gaming threshold** — There's no magic day where everything unlocks
- **No cliff arbitrage** — Can't time entries/exits around discrete steps
- **Proportional commitment** — Holding 6 months gets exactly half the benefit of 12 months
- **Natural exits** — Holders leave gradually when it makes economic sense, not in coordinated cliffs
